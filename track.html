For the purposes of transparency and community trust, we want to inform you that
this website tracks individual users upon thier access of the site.
Nothing about the user is stored, no identifying data is kept in any way
by anyone at What You Aut To Know. A cookie is stored on your own system by
the browser you are using simply saying "Yes, I've been here before"

The purpose of this is merely to clock the number of unique (different) visits
and traffic to the site to determin its use and effectivness for people.
What does this mean?

Put simply, once a user accesses the site for the first time, the counter goes up by one.
They can come back as many times as they like and the counter will stay the same.
It will go up if others visit, but it does not track repeated visits - only if you've been here before or not. Over time, a figure will be given of the overall amount of people who have accessed, and we can see from number that our potential reach. This holds true for the mobile version of the site as well.
